## Unit 1: Present Continuous (I am doing)

1. **Original:** I work on a new feature right now.
   **Corrected:** I **am working** on a new feature right now.
   *Note:* "Right now" signals present moment → Present Continuous.

2. **Original:** The developers test the API in the office.
   **Corrected:** The developers **are testing** the API in the office.
   *Note:* Action happening at this moment → Present Continuous.

3. **Original:** My colleague fix the critical bug today.
   **Corrected:** My colleague **is fixing** the critical bug today.
   *Note:* Ongoing action in the current period → Present Continuous.

---

## Unit 2: Present Simple (I do)

4. **Original:** She working as a software engineer.
   **Corrected:** She **works** as a software engineer.
   *Note:* General fact/permanent job → Present Simple.

5. **Original:** The company produce high-quality software.
   **Corrected:** The company **produces** high-quality software.
   *Note:* Habitual/general truth → Present Simple.

6. **Original:** Does he understand this code?
   **Corrected:** **Does he understand** this code? ✓
   *Note:* Correct question form for Present Simple.

---

## Unit 3: Present Continuous vs Simple 1 (Temporary vs Permanent)

7. **Original:** I live in Hanoi for the next year.
   **Corrected:** I **am living** in Hanoi for the next year.
   *Note:* "For the next year" = temporary situation → Continuous.

8. **Original:** My family is living in the same house for 20 years.
   **Corrected:** My family **lives** in the same house for 20 years.
   *Note:* "For 20 years" = permanent/long-term → Simple.

9. **Original:** I'm working here from Monday to Friday.
   **Corrected:** I **work** here from Monday to Friday. (Or "I'm **working** here this week" if truly temporary)
   *Note:* Regular schedule = Simple; temporary project = Continuous.

---

## Unit 4: Present Continuous vs Simple 2 (Stative Verbs)

10. **Original:** I am knowing the answer to this question.
    **Corrected:** I **know** the answer to this question.
    *Note:* "Know" is stative (state of mind) → Simple, not Continuous.

11. **Original:** What are you wanting to eat?
    **Corrected:** **What do you want** to eat?
    *Note:* "Want" describes desire (stative) → Simple.

12. **Original:** The document is containing 50 pages.
    **Corrected:** The document **contains** 50 pages.
    *Note:* "Contain" is stative → Simple.

13. **Original:** I am liking this programming language.
    **Corrected:** I **like** this programming language.
    *Note:* "Like" is stative (feeling/opinion) → Simple.

## Unit 5: Past Simple (I did)

14. **Original:** I finish my project yesterday.
    **Corrected:** I **finished** my project yesterday.
    *Note:* Completed action in past → Past Simple.

15. **Original:** They release the software last week.
    **Corrected:** They **released** the software last week.
    *Note:* "Last week" = definite past time → Past Simple.

16. **Original:** Did you attend the meeting yesterday?
    **Corrected:** **Did you attend** the meeting yesterday? ✓
    *Note:* Correct question form for Past Simple.

## Unit 6: Past Continuous (I was doing)

17. **Original:** I check the code when the power went off.
    **Corrected:** I **was checking** the code when the power went off.
    *Note:* Background action interrupted by another past event → Past Continuous + Past Simple.

18. **Original:** What did you do at 3 PM yesterday?
    **Corrected:** What **were you doing** at 3 PM yesterday?
    *Note:* Action in progress at a specific past time → Past Continuous.

19. **Original:** While she worked on the database, I design the UI.
    **Corrected:** While she **was working** on the database, I **was designing** the UI.
    *Note:* Two parallel actions in the past → both Past Continuous.

---

## Unit 7: Present Perfect 1 (I have done — Result Now)

20. **Original:** I lose my laptop.
    **Corrected:** I **have lost** my laptop.
    *Note:* Recent action with present result (I don't have it now) → Present Perfect.

21. **Original:** The team complete the project.
    **Corrected:** The team **has completed** the project.
    *Note:* Finished action; result visible now → Present Perfect.

22. **Original:** I update the software already.
    **Corrected:** I **have already updated** the software.
    *Note:* "Already" + recent completion → Present Perfect.

---

## Unit 8: Present Perfect 2 (I have done — Experience)

23. **Original:** Have you go to a tech conference before?
    **Corrected:** **Have you ever been to** a tech conference before?
    *Note:* Lifetime experience → Present Perfect + "ever".

24. **Original:** She worked at three different companies.
    **Corrected:** She **has worked** at three different companies.
    *Note:* Lifetime jobs (experience) → Present Perfect.

25. **Original:** I gone to this office many times.
    **Corrected:** I **have been to** this office many times.
    *Note:* "Been to" (experience of visiting) → Present Perfect.

---

## Unit 9: Present Perfect Continuous (I have been doing)

26. **Original:** I program this game for 5 hours.
    **Corrected:** I **have been programming** this game for 5 hours.
    *Note:* Duration in present (how long) → Present Perfect Continuous.

27. **Original:** They work on this API since morning.
    **Corrected:** They **have been working** on this API since morning.
    *Note:* "Since" + ongoing action → Present Perfect Continuous.

28. **Original:** She is developing new features for two weeks.
    **Corrected:** She **has been developing** new features for two weeks.
    *Note:* Forward-looking duration from past to now → Present Perfect Continuous.

---

## Unit 10: Present Perfect Continuous vs Simple (Process vs Result)

29. **Original:** I've been fixing this bug and I fix it.
    **Corrected:** I **have been fixing** this bug and **have finally fixed** it.
    *Note:* First = process (how long); second = final result.

30. **Original:** How long are you working on this project?
    **Corrected:** **How long have you been working** on this project?
    *Note:* "How long" in ongoing situation → Present Perfect Continuous.

31. **Original:** I have been writing 10 emails already.
    **Corrected:** I **have written** 10 emails already.
    *Note:* "Already" + countable result = Present Perfect Simple, not Continuous.

32. **Original:** The server is crashing for 3 days.
    **Corrected:** The server **has been crashing** for 3 days.
    *Note:* Duration of repeated problem → Present Perfect Continuous.

## Extended Practice: Mixed Scenarios (Units 1–10)

33. **Original:** I work here for 5 years and I still work here.
    **Corrected:** I **have been working** here for 5 years and I still **work** here.
    *Note:* First = duration to now (Cont); second = current state (Simple).

34. **Original:** When I arrive at the office, everyone work on their tasks.
    **Corrected:** When I arrived at the office, everyone **was working** on their tasks.
    *Note:* Coherence: past + background action → Past Continuous.

35. **Original:** I know this developer since we graduate together.
    **Corrected:** I **have known** this developer since we **graduated** together.
    *Note:* "Since + past event" = result extending to now → Present Perfect.

36. **Original:** Does the code run properly? — Yes, it run fine.
    **Corrected:** Does the code run properly? — Yes, it **runs** fine.
    *Note:* General present state → Present Simple.

37. **Original:** While the team is testing, the manager arrive.
    **Corrected:** While the team **was testing**, the manager **arrived**.
    *Note:* Coherence: both past → Past Continuous + Past Simple.

38. **Original:** She is liking to debug code and she debugging right now.
    **Corrected:** She **likes** to debug code and she's **debugging** right now.
    *Note:* First = general preference (Simple); second = this moment (Continuous).

39. **Original:** I attend conferences every year and I attend one last month.
    **Corrected:** I **attend** conferences every year and I **attended** one last month.
    *Note:* First = habit (Simple); second = past event (Past Simple).

40. **Original:** The database is containing sensitive data for years.
    **Corrected:** The database **has been containing** sensitive data for years. (Or: "The database **contains** sensitive data." depending on intent)
    *Note:* Stative verb "contain" rarely used in continuous; use Simple or show duration differently.

41. **Original:** I finish this feature. Can you review it?
    **Corrected:** I **have finished** this feature. Can you review it?
    *Note:* Recent completion with present relevance → Present Perfect.

42. **Original:** What do you do when the error appeared?
    **Corrected:** **What were you doing** when the error appeared?
    *Note:* Interrupted past action → Past Continuous + Past Simple.

43. **Original:** She work as a developer and she work on a new project now.
    **Corrected:** She **works** as a developer and she's **working** on a new project now.
    *Note:* First = job (Simple); second = current task (Continuous).

44. **Original:** I am knowing how to fix this, so I fixing it.
    **Corrected:** I **know** how to fix this, so I'm **fixing** it.
    *Note:* First = knowledge (Simple); second = action (Continuous).

45. **Original:** They have been develop this software for 2 years and they complete it finally.
    **Corrected:** They **have been developing** this software for 2 years and they **have finally completed** it.
    *Note:* First = long process (Continuous); second = result (Simple).
