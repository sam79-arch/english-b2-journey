## 📚 Core Grammar Reminder

| Form | Usage | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Present Continuous** | Temporary actions happening NOW | *I am working on a project* |
| **Present Simple** | Habits, permanent states, facts | *I work as a developer* |
| **Stative Verbs** | Mental/emotional states, perceptions, possession — NOT continuous | *I like/want/know/have/see* |
| **Dual-Meaning Verbs** | Some verbs change meaning in continuous form | *I think* (opinion) vs *I'm thinking* (considering) |

**1. Which is correct?**

**Correct Answer: ___**

**Model Explanation:** Sentence A is correct. "Think" is a stative verb expressing an opinion or belief, so it uses Present Simple. Sentence B is grammatically possible but changes the meaning — "am thinking" suggests the thought is occurring *right now* and may be temporary, which doesn't match the context of giving advice.

**2. Which is correct?**

A) She has a meeting with the director at 3 PM today.  
B) She is having a meeting with the director at 3 PM today.

**Correct Answer: ___**

**Student Explanation (1–2 sentences):**

_______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

**Model Explanation:** Both are technically correct, but B is better in this context. "Is having" emphasizes the action happening at a specific future time (3 PM today), while "has" is more neutral. In modern English, "is having" is preferred when discussing scheduled activities or temporary situations.

# SECTION E: Translation with Traps — Vietnamese → English (10 questions)

**Instructions:** Translate the Vietnamese sentences into English. Pay attention to the hints! Avoid the trap that the question warns you about.

---

**1.** "Tôi hiểu ý bạn rồi."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Stative verb "understand" — not "I am understanding"

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** I understand what you mean. / I see your point.  
**Explanation:** "Understand" is stative, so use Present Simple. Vietnamese doesn't distinguish between actions and states, so learners often incorrectly apply continuous form.

---

**2.** "Cô ấy đang suy nghĩ về việc bỏ việc."  
⚠️ **Careful:** "Thinking" can mean "considering" (temporary, use Continuous) or "opinion" (permanent, use Simple)

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** She is thinking about quitting her job. / She's considering quitting her job.  
**Explanation:** "Đang suy nghĩ" indicates a temporary mental process happening now, so use Present Continuous.

---

**3.** "Cái áo này cảm thấy mềm mại lắm."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Sensory verbs (feel, taste, smell) are usually stative when describing qualities

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** This shirt feels very soft.  
**Explanation:** "Feel" describing a tactile quality is stative. Use Simple form, not "is feeling."

---

**4.** "Bạn đang gặp bác sĩ không?"  
⚠️ **Careful:** Temporary action in progress vs. routine appointment

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** Are you seeing a doctor (now)? / Do you see a doctor (regularly)?  
**Explanation:** "Đang gặp" suggests an action happening right now, so use "Are you seeing" (Continuous). If it's a routine appointment "seeing a doctor" uses either form depending on context.

---

**5.** "Tôi có một cuồng chiếng mới."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Possession (have) vs. temporary situation (having)

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** I have a new guitar.  
**Explanation:** Permanent possession uses "have" (Simple). "I am having a guitar" is incorrect. Only use "having" for temporary situations like "having lunch."

---

**6.** "Anh ấy đang nói tiếng Anh với những khách hàng."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Action in progress (Continuous) not habit (Simple)

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** He is speaking English with the customers.  
**Explanation:** "Đang nói" indicates an action happening right now, so use Present Continuous. "He speaks English" would mean it's his language skill or habit.

---

**7.** "Bát súp này có vị ngon lắm."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Describing quality (Simple) vs. someone actively tasting (Continuous)

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** This soup tastes delicious.  
**Explanation:** "Có vị" describes an inherent quality, so use Simple form. "Is tasting" would mean someone is in the act of tasting the soup.

---

**8.** "Tôi không tin những gì anh ấy nói."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Opinion/belief (Simple) not temporary thought (Continuous)

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** I don't believe what he says.  
**Explanation:** "Không tin" expresses disbelief as an opinion/state, so use Simple. "Am not believing" is unnatural.

---

**9.** "Cô ấy sống ở Hà Nội từ năm 2020."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Permanent state (Simple) — not action in progress

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** She lives in Hanoi since 2020. / She has lived in Hanoi since 2020.  
**Explanation:** Use Simple form for permanent residence. Note: In this context, Present Perfect "has lived" is actually more natural for the duration, but "lives" is grammatically acceptable for the state.

**10.** "Bây giờ tôi đang cân nhắc lời đề nghị việc làm của họ."  
⚠️ **Careful:** Action happening right now (Continuous) with temporary duration

**Your Answer: _______________**

**Correct Answer:** Right now, I'm considering their job offer. / I'm thinking about their job offer.  
**Explanation:** "Đang cân nhắc" describes an action in process at this moment, so use Present Continuous "am considering" or "am thinking about."
