## KEY FOCUS for Days 11-13

### **Unit 9: Perfect Continuous (I have been doing)** 🟥

Use for:
- **Process started in past, continues to present**
- **How long?** (duration from past until now)
- **Emphasis on PROCESS, not result**

**Examples:**
- "I **have been working** since 8 AM" ✅ (still working)
- "How long **have you been studying**?" ✅ (duration)
- "She **has been developing** for 3 hours" ✅ (ongoing process)

**Pattern:** `have/has + been + -ing form`

---

### **Unit 10: Perfect Continuous vs Simple** 🟥

**Perfect Continuous = Process (how long)**
```
"I have been working for 3 hours"
(still working, focus on duration)
```

**Perfect Simple = Result**
```
"I have completed 6 tasks"
(finished, focus on number)
```

**VALID MIX:** Perfect Continuous + Past Simple
```
"They have been testing and found bugs" ✅
(process = continuous, result = simple)
```

---

### **Stative Verb Reminder** ⚠️

From Day 08 — these never take continuous:
- like, want, know, understand, prefer, love, hate, own, belong, contain, seem, sound, taste, appear...

❌ "I am wanting coffee"  
✅ "I want coffee"
