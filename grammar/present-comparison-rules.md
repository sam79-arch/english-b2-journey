# Unit 3 & 4: Present Continuous vs Present Simple (Summary from GIU 5th Ed)

## Unit 3: Temporary vs. Permanent

| Aspect | Present Continuous | Present Simple |
|---|---|---|
| **Use** | For **temporary** situations | For **permanent** situations |
| **Logic** | Things happening for a short time around now | Things that are always true or happen for a long time |
| **Example 1** | "I’m living with some friends until I find a place of my own." | "My parents live in London. They have lived there all their lives." |
| **Example 2** | "You’re working hard today." | "Joe isn’t lazy. He works hard most of the time." |

## Unit 4: Stative Verbs (Thoughts, Feelings, etc.)

Some verbs are **not normally used** in the continuous form because they describe a state, not an action.

### Common Stative Verbs:
*   **Thoughts/Knowledge:** `believe`, `know`, `realize`, `suppose`, `understand`, `remember`, `forget`, `mean`
*   **Feelings:** `like`, `love`, `hate`, `want`, `need`, `prefer`, `mind`
*   **Senses/Appearance:** `seem`, `hear`, `see`, `look`, `sound`
*   **Possession/Other:** `belong`, `contain`, `consist`, `depend`, `fit`, `own`, `matter`

### Examples:
*   **Correct:** "I **know** what you mean." (Not *I am knowing*)
*   **Correct:** "Does this book **belong** to you?" (Not *Is this book belonging*)
*   **Correct:** "I **see** a man over there." (Not *I am seeing* - though *see* can be used in some contexts, GIU Unit 4 focuses on the stative use).

### Special Case: Think
*   **As a belief:** "I **think** Mary is Canadian." (Simple)
*   **As a process of considering:** "I **am thinking** about what happened." (Continuous)
