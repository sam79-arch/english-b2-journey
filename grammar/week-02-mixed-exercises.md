### Days 08–13 (03-12 to 03-17): Intensive Mixed Exercises 🔥

#### Format for Each Day:

**Total Time: 2–3 hours/day (flexible)**

1. **Warm-up (10 min):** Read yesterday's Bug Log
2. **Mixed Exercise Block 1 (50 min):** 50 mixed sentences (randomized from all 10 units) — Difficulty: Easy to Medium
3. **Break (10 min):** Rest & hydrate
4. **Mixed Exercise Block 2 (50 min):** 50 more mixed sentences (harder difficulty) — Difficulty: Medium to Hard
5. **Correction & Logging (20 min):** Write down errors → bug log, identify patterns

---

#### Exercise Difficulty Levels:

**Level 1 (Easy):** Single unit focus within mixed context
- Example: "Identify if each sentence uses correct Present Continuous or another tense"

**Level 2 (Medium):** 2–3 units per exercise
- Example: "Complete the sentence using correct tense (could be Present Simple, Present Continuous, or Past Simple)"

**Level 3 (Hard):** 4+ units mixed, requires deep reasoning
- Example: "Rewrite the story in past tense, maintaining correct aspect and continuity"

---

### Day 08 (03-12): Present Tenses Focus (Units 1–4)

**Main theme:** Present Continuous vs Simple, Stative verbs

**Total: 100 mixed sentences**

**Exercise structure:**
- Block 1 (50 sentences): Correct present tense errors, fill-in-the-blank, rewrite exercises
- Block 2 (50 sentences): Harder scenarios mixing Units 1–4 with other tenses

**Sample exercise:**
```
Correct these sentences (mix of Units 1–4):
1. I am knowing the answer.
2. What you do at the moment?
3. She is working here for 10 years.
4. They choosing a new office location.
5. Does he understand what I mean?
```

---

### Day 09 (03-13): Past Tenses Focus (Units 5–6)

**Main theme:** Past Simple vs Past Continuous, background actions

**Total: 100 mixed sentences**

**Exercise structure:**
- Block 1 (50 sentences): Correct past tense errors, story completion, fill-in-the-blank
- Block 2 (50 sentences): Harder scenarios with narrative rewriting and complex situations

**Sample exercise:**
```
Complete with Past Simple or Past Continuous:
1. While I ____________ (work) on the code, the server ____________ (crash).
2. What ____________ (you / do) when the error ____________ (happen)?
3. She ____________ (fix) bugs for 2 hours yesterday.
4. They ____________ (release) the software while we ____________ (test) it.
```

---

### Day 10 (03-14): Present Perfect Focus (Units 7–10)

**Main theme:** Present Perfect Simple vs Continuous, result vs process

**Total: 100 mixed sentences**

**Exercise structure:**
- Block 1 (50 sentences): Present Perfect variations, error correction, fill-in-the-blank
- Block 2 (50 sentences): Complex scenarios mixing Units 7–10 with other tenses, narrative exercises

**Sample exercise:**
```
Choose Present Perfect Simple or Continuous:
1. I ____________ (learn / learning) programming for 3 years.
2. We ____________ (develop / developed) 5 software projects so far.
3. How long ____________ (you / work) on this feature?
4. The team ____________ (just / finish) the database migration.
```

---

### Days 11–13 (03-15 to 03-17): Cumulative Mixed Exercises 🎯

**All 10 units together — maximum difficulty**

**Structure per day: 100 mixed sentences**
- Block 1 (50 sentences): Mixed difficulty, all units randomized
- Block 2 (50 sentences): Advanced scenarios requiring deep understanding across units
- Short diagnostic at end of each day
- Identify remaining weak spots

**Hard Exercise Example:**
```
Rewrite the following story in past tense, then answer questions about it:

[Original in mixed tenses]
"I am working on a feature and I have been thinking about the best approach. 
My colleague suggests using a new library because she knows it works well. 
I agree because I have heard good things about it."

Task:
a) Rewrite in pure past tense
b) Identify which tenses you used
c) Explain why you chose each tense
```
